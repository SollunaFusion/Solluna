Teachers



Alan Best

Alan was a regular in the Contra dance community throughout his childhood. Alan has been an organizer, teacher, musician and dancer for the triangle’s favorite Waltz Nights for last several years. Six years ago, Alan branched out into the world of Blues and Fusion dance and began teaching four years ago. Alan’s excitement for dance brightens a room. You can expect to leave Alan’s lesson with a smile ready to have fun on the dance floor.


Benja

Benja Newnam is a contemporary choreographer and performer from Durham, NC. With over 10 years of experience studying and teaching various social dances and contemporary concert dance, he most appreciates the connection and communication specifically found in partner dancing. Freshly back home from a summer in West Virginia working with TWV professional theatre company, he hopes to share the important marriage of performance and listening in workshops for the social dance floor.


Beth Butler

Dancing has always felt like the most natural state of being for Beth. Growing up, she danced her way across living rooms, gym courts, and theater stages. Beth started dancing Lindy Hop college and quickly fell in love with social dance. Three years ago, Beth began dancing blues and fusion where she experienced blissful connections and new freedom. In her classes, she would like to inspire deeper connections and foster creative movements so that dancers feel closer to themselves and those around them.


Lydie Costes

At age six, Lydie wrote the following poem: “First you get a twirly skirt. Then you move your feet. Now you’re dancing!” She grew up dancing jazz, contemporary, and other solo dances, and stumbled across latin partner dancing in high school. Once she discovered the joys of social dancing there was no going back. She is certified to teach Brazilian Zouk and directs the Triangle Zouk scene. She brings additional influence to her fusion lessons from a variety of social dances, including blues, salsa, kizomba, contact improv, and tango. She believes that dance is one of the best forms of exercise for mind, body, and soul, and hopes to inspire others to connect in deeper ways.


Robert Pehlman


Shane Clark

Shane Clark is a Philly native who grew up in the exciting world of Contra dancing, where she developed a love for live music, community, and storytelling. Shane has been teaching social dance since 2011 and brings techniques from Blues, Swing, Tango, Kizomba, House, and Contact Improv to the Fusion dance floor. Shane focuses on creating classes that are accessible, fun, and empowering for everyone. She hopes people will leave each of her classes with heightened curiosity, and a deeper sense self-efficacy as learners, as dancers, and as members of a community.



DJs



Aaron Hawkins


Kyrstal Beth

Kyrstal Beth found her DJ start at Durham house parties and has become a regular DJ at RDU Saturday Night Fusion. The freedom of Fusion dancing lead her to search for music that inspired the freedom of movements. She traveled around the US and Europe collecting music to connect through. From flowing melodies to trap beats, Kyrstal Beth will bring you tunes to set you free.


Bo Marchman

Raised in the Durham blues and fusion scene, Bo has spent the last several years traveling, dancing and collecting music around the world. Drawn to organic rhythms, percussive beats and candlelit voices, he weaves traditional sounds and contemporary genres together into an intimate dance experience. Currently based in Philadelphia, he has spun tunes for international fusion events and won the DJ battle at the DJ Experiment in 2017.


Phoebe Thatcher

Chicago MP3 queen Phoebe has dropped hot sets from coast to coast and is thrilled to bring her unique blend of moody RnB and luscious bass to the Triangle. Expect steel drums, brain-rattling 808s, and just shy of too much Rihanna.
