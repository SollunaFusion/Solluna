Safe Spaces Policy


Why safer spaces?

Social dancing requires a unique amount of vulnerability and trust. At SolLuna, we strive to create a space in which all participants feel both empowered to express themselves and safe connecting with others. In light of this, we ask that all participants act with an awareness of and consideration for those around them.

We cannot prevent all acts of harassment and bigotry, but we can create a culture that is fundamentally opposed to such expressions of violence. Together, we can build a culture founded on compassion, respect, and support.

We need your help to make this happen!

Please always:

  • Obtain consent before dancing or touching another person, unless you have a priorly-negotiated agreement with that individual (see “What does consent look like?” below)
  • Say “no” when you want to say “no.” This applies on and off the dance floor, no explanation required.
  • Communicate when someone is making you feel uncomfortable, either by directly addressing the individual or by seeking the help of an organizer.
  • Seek help from a friend or an organizer if someone has hurt you.
  • ake care of others - call out/report actions that are harming the emotional or physical safety of others.

In order to create a more open and inclusive culture, we also ask that dancers:

  • Ask before each dance if your partner would like to lead or follow or express that you are most comfortable in one of those roles. Please do not assume another person’s preferred dance role.
  • Ask someone you don’t know to dance. We welcome new folks - whether new dancers or new to this event - into the SolLuna community!

What does consent look like?

Consent may be verbal (e.g. “Would you care to dance?” “Would you like a hug?”) or nonverbal (an outstretched hand). If an invitation is unclear or if another person is not acknowledging a nonverbal “no,” please clarify using clear spoken language.

Who is welcome at SolLuna?

At SolLuna, we welcome all humans, regardless of their race, ethnic background, body type, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, religion, socioeconomic position, or age. However, we recognize that - despite our efforts and intentions - all people may not feel welcome in our community. Please let an organizer know if you have experienced bigotry or discrimination during your time spent at SolLuna.

Accessibility at SolLuna

The majority of this event will take place on a grassy field. Please contact the organizers (sollunafusion@gmail.com) if you have questions about accessibility or would like to request accommodations.
